Kick counting

How to count baby kicks

Practical guidance for personal pregnancy tracking — then log sessions in Preglo when you are ready.

What kick counting is

Kick counting means noticing fetal movements — rolls, jabs, flutters — during a quiet, timed window so you learn your baby’s usual pattern.

When to start

Many clinicians suggest focusing on movements in the third trimester (often from around week 28). Follow your provider’s advice if you have a higher-risk pregnancy.

A simple everyday method

  1. Pick a consistent time when baby is usually active.
  2. Lie on your side or sit comfortably in a quiet place.
  3. Count each distinct movement until you reach 10, or use the timed window your clinician recommends.
  4. Note how long it took and how it compared to recent days.

Track sessions in Preglo

Preglo’s baby kick counter lets you start a session, tap for each movement, and keep history next to your week tracker. See also the 10-kick count method and guidance on reduced fetal movement.

If movements feel much weaker or fewer than your baby’s normal pattern, contact your care provider. Preglo is not a diagnostic tool.

FAQ

When should I count kicks?

Often in the third trimester, at a consistent quiet time — follow your clinician’s advice.

What counts as a kick?

Rolls, jabs, and clear flutters usually count; hiccups may be noted separately depending on guidance.
