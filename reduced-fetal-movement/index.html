Kick awareness

Reduced fetal movement

Practical guidance for personal pregnancy tracking — written to be clear, calm, and honest about limits.

Why movement patterns matter more than one dramatic kick

Babies have quieter hours and busier hours. A single still stretch can be sleep. What clinicians care about is a clear change from your baby’s usual pattern — fewer movements, weaker movements, or a day that simply feels wrong to you.

A practical same-day response

  1. Find a quiet place and lie on your side if you can.
  2. Focus for a short, intentional window — many parents start a kick session to stay honest about what they feel.
  3. If movements still feel reduced, or you remain worried, contact your midwife, doctor, or maternity triage the same day.
  4. Do not wait overnight “just to see” if your instincts say something is different.

What Preglo can and cannot do

Preglo stores kick sessions so you remember recent patterns without scrolling chat screenshots. That history can support a clearer conversation with your care team. It does not diagnose placental issues, oxygen levels, or fetal distress, and it should never delay a phone call.

Build a healthier baseline before you need it

Learn a simple routine in how to count baby kicks, understand the 10-kick method, and keep sessions inside the Preglo kick counter so your week tracker and movement log live together.

Seek medical help promptly for reduced movements. This page is not emergency guidance and is not a substitute for clinical assessment.

FAQ

Should I wait until tomorrow?

If you are concerned about reduced movements, contact your care provider the same day — do not delay for an app score.

Can stress change what I feel?

Position, meals, and baby sleep cycles change sensation, but worry itself is a valid reason to check with a clinician.

Is no movement for an hour always an emergency?

Babies sleep. Use your personal pattern and your clinician’s instructions; when unsure, call rather than negotiate with yourself.
