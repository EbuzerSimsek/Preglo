Baby development at week 11

The skeleton begins ossifying. Hair follicles and nail beds appear. Diaphragm practice and hiccup-like motions can start even if you cannot feel them.

Average size estimates for week 11 are about 4.1 cm and 7 g — roughly fig. Your ultrasound report and clinician’s dating override any website average.

What you might feel

Round ligament twinges or bloating can appear. Clothes may feel different before a clear bump.

You are in the first trimester. Two pregnancies at the same week can feel completely different — treat these notes as orientation, not a checklist you must match.

What to track this week

  • Symptoms, mood, sleep, and energy in your daily log
  • Water intake toward a simple daily goal
  • Appointment dates and questions for your care provider

Week 11 FAQ

How big is the baby at week 11?

Typical educational averages are about 4.1 cm long and 7 g. Individual growth and measurement methods vary.

Which trimester is week 11?

Week 11 is generally counted in the first trimester (weeks 1–12 first, 13–27 second, 28–40 third in common educational grouping).
