Baby development at week 3

The blastocyst may attach to the uterine lining. Tiny hormonal shifts start, but they are often too early for clear symptoms or a reliable test.

Average size estimates for week 3 are about still microscopic and too early to weigh meaningfully — roughly poppy seed stage. Your ultrasound report and clinician’s dating override any website average.

What you might feel

Mild cramping or spotting can happen with implantation — or with a regular cycle. Do not assume either way without a test and clinical context.

You are in the first trimester. Two pregnancies at the same week can feel completely different — treat these notes as orientation, not a checklist you must match.

What to track this week

  • Symptoms, mood, sleep, and energy in your daily log
  • Water intake toward a simple daily goal
  • Appointment dates and questions for your care provider

Week 3 FAQ

How big is the baby at week 3?

Typical educational averages are about still microscopic long and too early to weigh meaningfully. Individual growth and measurement methods vary.

Which trimester is week 3?

Week 3 is generally counted in the first trimester (weeks 1–12 first, 13–27 second, 28–40 third in common educational grouping).
