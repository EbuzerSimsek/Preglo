Baby development at week 1

Clinically, week 1 is often the week of your last menstrual period. Conception has not happened yet for most people; the calendar is simply starting so clinicians can estimate a due date later.

Average size estimates for week 1 are about still microscopic and too early to weigh meaningfully — roughly poppy seed stage. Your ultrasound report and clinician’s dating override any website average.

What you might feel

You may feel like your usual cycle. There is nothing pregnancy-specific to track yet unless you are actively trying to conceive and logging periods.

You are in the first trimester. Two pregnancies at the same week can feel completely different — treat these notes as orientation, not a checklist you must match.

What to track this week

  • Symptoms, mood, sleep, and energy in your daily log
  • Water intake toward a simple daily goal
  • Appointment dates and questions for your care provider

Week 1 FAQ

How big is the baby at week 1?

Typical educational averages are about still microscopic long and too early to weigh meaningfully. Individual growth and measurement methods vary.

Which trimester is week 1?

Week 1 is generally counted in the first trimester (weeks 1–12 first, 13–27 second, 28–40 third in common educational grouping).
