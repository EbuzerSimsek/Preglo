Kick counting

The 10-kick count method

Practical guidance for personal pregnancy tracking — then log sessions in Preglo when you are ready.

What the 10-kick method is

The 10-kick count (sometimes called “count-to-10”) asks you to note how long it takes to feel about 10 distinct movements in one sitting.

Why people use it

It creates a personal baseline. The goal is not a universal number of minutes — it is noticing a clear change from your baby’s usual timing.

How to do it

  • Choose a calm moment, often after a snack or in the evening.
  • Start a timer and count movements until you reach 10.
  • Save the duration in Preglo so you can compare days.

Read how to count baby kicks for positioning tips, or reduced fetal movement if something feels off.

Always follow your clinician’s preferred protocol. This page is educational only.

FAQ

How long should 10 kicks take?

It varies by baby. Track your own average and ask your provider what threshold they want you to use.

Do hiccups count?

Ask your care provider — some protocols exclude hiccups.
